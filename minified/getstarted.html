Launching Balloons | Get Started

Here's what you need to know.

Launching balloons can be a very exciting and fun experience. It can also be very dangerous if the proper precautions are not taken prior to launch. The information below can be used as a beginner’s guide, but we strongly suggest that you also go over proper handling techniques for compressed gas and talk in depth with an FAA official about the potential risks involved.

Before You Start

  1. Determine your launch date and contact the FAA regarding the launch. This is not normally required for smaller balloon launches, but it is definitely strongly recommended. They will go over your local laws about launching balloons like this as well.
  2. Find a local gas supplier and read their safety information about Hydrogen and the risks associated with its usage. Hydrogen is extremely flammable and it is very important to know how to handle it properly.
  3. Try to avoid launching alone. Get a group of friends involved. This is a fun project and it's easier to manage with the extra hands if any problems arise.

Step 3: Selecting Your Launch Location

When selecting your location it is important to keep 3 things in mind.

Keep the location open

Ensure that there is nothing for the balloon payload to bump into. Choose a spot away from buildings, trees, powerlines, etc. Generally open fields or the tops of parking decks have been our winning locations.

Let your location know

Notifying someone that you’re launching from this site is very important. If you just roll up with a large tank of compressed hydrogen, people are going to have a problem. A quick call and explanation can mean the difference between a smooth launch and a trip to the police station.

Avoid areas near airports

You are essentially sending an object up to heights where commercial airliners fly. This means you need to avoid launch sites near airports and airstrips to avoid serious complications.

Conclusion: Ask Questions and Have a Good Time

At this point you are ready to get rolling. After launching your balloon, jump on the forum and share with us. We love seeing other launches, whether they are successful or not. If you have any questions about which balloon to use or problems you encounter, getting online and asking is the easiest and quickest way to get those issues resolved. Collaboration is the best way to solve complex problems and the community is full of people who want to help you succeed.
