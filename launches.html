Launches

Below you will find comprehensive data from our first three launches. We didn’t get any data from our first launch due to our faulty launch method, but each subsequent launch has provided more scientific data. This progression is outlined below, showing exactly how much we’ve grown through our multiple launches.

Launch Number 1

Weather Balloon Launch from Cole Gentry on Vimeo.

Our first and most rookie launch consisted of a payload with just a camera and GPS tracker. We kept it simple for this one, with our only goal being to get some visual data and to reclaim our assets. It was extremely successful launch and gave us an opportunity to learn a great deal about balloon launching and retrieval.

Launch Number 2

Weather Balloon Launch No. 2 from Cole Gentry on Vimeo.

Our payload on the first launch experienced a lot of spinning and shaking so the video wasn’t as clean as we’d have liked. Our next goal then was to make our payload more stable so we’d have better quality video results. In addition, we added a small piece of hardware from a company called Hexpert Systems. This little on-board computer allowed us to track our altitude as well as temperature and pressure data throughout the flight. Unfortunately our new method of payload design did not provide us with any sort of help in relation to our stabilization problems. So we had to go back to the drawing board on that. On the upside, however, we were able to pull some of the interesting data listed below.

Launch Number Number 3

100,000 Ft Balloon Journey from Cole Gentry on Vimeo.

After our first two launches, we really wanted to do something to set this next launch apart from the rest. We wanted quality HD footage as well as some serious logging data this time. After some serious research and development we created our most efficient payload to date. This unit had exponentially less wind resistance and focused closely on weight distribution. It also had increased tether lengths between the balloon itself and the payload. All of these factors combined provided us with the best video footage we have ever captured. To bring more scientific data back we also added a GPS logger in addition to our GPS tracker. The logger plotted our data for the whole flight so we could see exactly where the balloon had traveled.
